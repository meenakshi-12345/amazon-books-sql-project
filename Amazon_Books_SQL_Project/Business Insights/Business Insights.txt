1. Fiction vs Non-Fiction Pricing

The project compares average prices between Fiction and Non-Fiction books to understand category pricing trends.

Insight:

Non-Fiction books generally tend to have higher prices due to educational and informational value.

2. Customer Engagement Analysis

Review counts were used as a proxy for customer engagement.

Insight:

Books with massive review counts usually indicate:

Higher popularity
Better market reach
Strong customer trust
3. Publisher Performance

Publisher-level analysis identified which publishers:

Produce the most bestselling books
Receive the highest customer reviews
Maintain high ratings
Insight:

A small number of publishers dominate bestseller categories consistently.

4. Pricing vs Ratings

The project investigates whether expensive books are highly rated.

Insight:

Higher pricing does not always guarantee higher ratings.
Affordable books can also achieve excellent customer satisfaction.

5. Bestseller Longevity

Weeks on bestseller list were analyzed to identify long-term successful books.

Insight:

Books with longer bestseller durations likely have:

Strong word-of-mouth marketing
Consistent demand
High reader retention
6. Category Dominance

The project identifies which categories attract maximum reviews and engagement.

Insight:

Popular categories tend to dominate both reviews and sales visibility.
